Agreement Terms
1 Definitions
For the purposes of this Agreement:
  • "Agreement" — This Master Export Sales Agreement including all Schedules and Annexures.
  • "Seller" — Siva Electronics & Home Appliances, a proprietorship firm registered under the laws of India.
  • "Buyer" — Daniel R. Fitzgerald, an individual/entity domiciled in Austin, Texas, USA.
  • "Goods" — Consumer Electronics and Home Appliances as specified in individual PIs issued under this Agreement.
  • "PI" — A Proforma Invoice issued by the Seller specifying goods, quantity, price, and terms for a specific order.
  • "PO" — A Purchase Order issued by the Buyer accepting the terms of a PI.
  • "Advance" — The 30% upfront payment made by Buyer to confirm an order.
  • "Shipment Date" — The date on which FedEx or the nominated courier collects the goods from the Seller's premises.
  • "FEMA" — The Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 of India.
  • "CIF" — Cost, Insurance, and Freight as defined under Incoterms 2020.
2 Scope & Duration
2.1 This Agreement governs all export transactions of Goods between the Seller and Buyer from the effective date of April 20, 2026 and shall remain valid for 24 months unless earlier terminated under Clause 18.

2.2 Each transaction shall be governed by an individual PI issued by the Seller and accepted by the Buyer via a signed PO or written confirmation.

2.3 In case of conflict between this Agreement and an individual PI/PO, the terms of this Agreement shall prevail unless the PI/PO expressly states otherwise in writing with mutual consent.
3 Products & Specifications
3.1 The Seller shall supply Consumer Electronics and Home Appliances including but not limited to: LED/Smart TVs, Air Conditioners, Ceiling Fans, Induction Cooktops, Water Purifiers, and other BEE-rated appliances as specified in individual PIs.

3.2 All Goods shall conform to the following standards:
  • BEE (Bureau of Energy Efficiency) certified where applicable.
  • ISI marked (Bureau of Indian Standards) for applicable categories.
  • US FCC / UL safety compliance certifications available on request.
  • Free from manufacturing defects at time of dispatch.
  • Packed in original manufacturer packaging with intact serial numbers.
3.3 Seller shall provide a Test Report / Certificate of Conformity for each product category upon request within 5 business days of shipment.

3.4 Seller reserves the right to substitute an equivalent model if a specific variant is unavailable due to manufacturing constraints, with prior written notification of at least 5 business days.
4 Pricing & Quotation
4.1 All prices shall be quoted and invoiced in United States Dollars (USD) on a CIF (Cost, Insurance & Freight) basis to the port of destination specified in the individual PI.

4.2 Prices quoted in a PI are fixed and binding for the validity period of that PI (typically 30 days from PI date).

4.3 For subsequent orders, Seller reserves the right to revise pricing based on:
  • Component cost fluctuations (semiconductors, copper, steel)
  • Changes in freight rates by FedEx or nominated carrier
  • Currency exchange rate movements exceeding 5% from the PI date
  • Government-imposed levies, export duties, or cess
4.4 Any price revision shall be communicated by the Seller with 7 days written notice. Buyer retains the right to decline a revised PI without penalty.
5 Payment Terms
5.1 Standard payment terms for all orders under this Agreement:
  • 30% Advance: Payable within 5 business days of Buyer's acceptance of PI. No production/procurement shall commence until advance is received.
  • 70% Balance: Payable prior to dispatch. Seller shall notify Buyer that goods are ready and share pre-shipment photographs. Balance must be received within 3 business days of such notification.
5.2 All payments shall be made via Secure Online Payment Link shared by the Seller per order via email:

5.4 Payment gateway transaction charges, if any, are for the Buyer's account.

5.5 Payments made in currencies other than USD shall be converted at the prevailing exchange rate applied by the payment gateway at the time of transaction.
6 Order Confirmation Process
6.1 The order process under this Agreement shall follow these steps:
  • Step 1 — PI Issuance: Seller issues a Proforma Invoice detailing goods, quantity, price, and terms.
  • Step 2 — PI Acceptance: Buyer signs and returns the PI within 5 business days. Unsigned PI = no order confirmed.
  • Step 3 — Advance Payment: Buyer pays 30% advance via the secure payment link shared by Seller within 5 business days of signed PI.
  • Step 4 — PO Issuance: Buyer issues a formal Purchase Order referencing the PI number.
  • Step 5 — Production & Packaging: Seller commences procurement/packaging upon receipt of advance. Lead time: 7–10 business days.
  • Step 6 — Pre-Shipment Notification: Seller notifies Buyer with photos of packed goods and invoice for balance payment.
  • Step 7 — Balance Payment & Dispatch: Buyer pays 70% balance. Seller dispatches within 2 business days of payment confirmation.
  • Step 8 — Document Dispatch: Seller sends all export documents (CI, Packing List, AWB, COO, e-FIRC) via email within 24 hours of shipment.
7 Delivery & Incoterms
7.1 Default delivery terms: CIF (Cost, Insurance & Freight) – Incoterms 2020. Seller bears all costs and risks to load the goods aboard the carrier at the port of export, including freight and insurance to the port of destination.

7.2 Risk transfers from Seller to Buyer upon delivery to port of discharge as per the AWB / Bill of Lading.

7.3 Standard shipment route: Chennai Sea Port (INMAS1), India → Port of Houston, TX, USA via FedEx International Priority Air Freight.

7.4 Estimated transit time: 5–8 business days from shipment date. Seller shall not be liable for delays caused by carrier, customs clearance, or force majeure events.

7.5 Seller shall book shipment only with Tier-1 carriers (FedEx, DHL, UPS) whose tracking numbers are internationally recognized. Tracking details shall be shared with Buyer on the day of dispatch.
8 Export Documentation
8.1 For every shipment, Seller shall provide the following documents to Buyer via email:
  • Commercial Invoice — final billing document with actual shipped quantities
  • Packing List — carton-by-carton breakdown with weights and dimensions
  • Airway Bill (AWB) — carrier's receipt and proof of shipment
  • Courier Shipping Bill (CSB-V) — filed as "Commercial Export", not gift/sample
  • Certificate of Origin (COO) — issued by FIEO/Chamber of Commerce
  • e-FIRC — Foreign Inward Remittance Certificate issued by payment gateway post-settlement
  • Test Reports / BEE/ISI Certificates — on request, within 5 business days
8.2 All documents shall be in English and compliant with US Customs and Border Protection (CBP) import requirements.
9 Inspection, Quality & Claims
9.1 Seller warrants that all Goods supplied meet the quality and safety standards described in Clause 3 and the applicable PI.

9.2 Buyer shall inspect the Goods within 5 business days of receipt at destination. If no complaint is raised within this period, the Goods are deemed accepted.

9.3 Any quality claim must be:
  • Raised in writing to sivaelectronicsandhomeappliances@gmail.com within 7 days of receipt
  • Accompanied by clear photographic/video evidence of the defect or damage
  • Accompanied by the original packaging and serial number visible
9.4 Seller's liability is limited to, at Seller's sole discretion: replacement of defective Goods; or credit note for future order; or partial refund not exceeding the invoiced value of defective Goods.

9.5 Seller shall not be liable for damage caused by improper installation, misuse, voltage fluctuations, or customs inspection after delivery.
10 Packaging & Labelling
10.1 All Goods shall be packed in original manufacturer packaging with:
  • Double-wall corrugated outer export cartons with foam padding
  • Fragile/Handle With Care markings on all cartons
  • Gross/Net weight, serial number, model number, and country of origin on each carton
  • Anti-static packaging for sensitive electronics where applicable
10.2 Labels shall comply with US FCC labelling requirements including model number, manufacturer details, voltage/frequency rating, and safety certifications.

10.3 Buyer may request custom or private-label packaging for orders above $5,000 USD with a lead time of 14–21 business days, subject to a one-time design approval process.
11 Force Majeure
11.1 Neither party shall be liable for failure or delay in performance if caused by events beyond reasonable control, including: Acts of God, government actions, port closures, carrier strikes, pandemics, power failures, or cyber attacks.

11.2 The affected party shall notify the other within 48 hours of the force majeure event.

11.3 If a force majeure event continues for more than 30 consecutive days, either party may terminate the affected order without liability, subject to refund of advance payments for unshipped goods.

18 Term & Termination
18.1 This Agreement commences on April 20, 2026 and shall remain in force for 24 months, automatically renewing for successive 12-month periods unless either party provides written notice of non-renewal at least 30 days before renewal.

18.2 Either party may terminate with 30 days written notice. Termination shall not affect obligations under any outstanding PO.

18.3 Either party may terminate immediately if: the other party commits a material breach uncured for 15 days; becomes insolvent or ceases to carry on business; or a force majeure event continues for more than 60 consecutive days.

18.4 Upon termination: all outstanding amounts become immediately payable; advance payments for unshipped goods shall be refunded within 15 business days; each party shall return or destroy confidential information of the other party.
